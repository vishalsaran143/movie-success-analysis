# Budget vs ROI
<img width="1340" height="525" alt="budget_roi" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/602bfca7-d58b-41a2-982d-b1b5f17996e9" />

**Insight**
<br>
- When the budget is low (0–50M), ROI can be extremely high — up to 1000–2000%. This means small-budget movies can generate huge returns if they succeed.
- As the budget increases (100M+), ROI becomes more stable at around 100–300%. This indicates that high-budget films are safer investments but offer lower returns.

- In short: low budget = high risk, high reward; high budget = lower risk, stable returns.
